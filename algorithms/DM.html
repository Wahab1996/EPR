🔷 Summary

Diabetes mellitus (DM) is a group of metabolic diseases characterized by chronic hyperglycemia. Type 1 is autoimmune, with absolute insulin deficiency. Type 2 involves insulin resistance with relative deficiency, strongly linked to obesity and lifestyle. Diagnosis is based on glucose or HbA1c levels, and management is comprehensive, addressing glycemic control and associated risks.

🔷 Types & Comparison

Type 1 DM: Autoimmune β-cell destruction, usually childhood onset, high risk of ketoacidosis. Requires insulin.

Type 2 DM: Insulin resistance & β-cell dysfunction, adult onset, often asymptomatic. Managed with lifestyle, oral agents, and possibly insulin.

🔷 Epidemiology

T1DM: ~5-10% of diabetics, peak at 4–6 & 10–14 years, more common in Whites.
T2DM: ~90%, increasing prevalence, more common in older adults, certain minorities (Native American, Hispanic, African American, Asian).

🔷 Etiology

T1DM: HLA-DR3/DR4 positive, linked to other autoimmune diseases (e.g., Hashimoto).
T2DM: Risk factors include obesity, sedentary lifestyle, family history, high-risk ethnicity, and certain medications (e.g., glucocorticoids).

🔷 Clinical Features

Classic hyperglycemia symptoms: Polyuria, polydipsia, polyphagia, weight loss. T1DM often acute; T2DM gradual and often asymptomatic.

🔷 Screening & Diagnosis

Recommended for high-risk individuals (age ≥35, obesity + risk factors). Diagnostic tests: FPG ≥126 mg/dL, HbA1c ≥6.5%, OGTT ≥200 mg/dL, or random glucose ≥200 mg/dL with symptoms.

🔷 Management

Goals: Achieve and maintain glycemic targets while minimizing hypoglycemia. Key components:

  • 🩺 Lifestyle: diet, exercise, smoking cessation.
  • 💊 Medications: Metformin first-line for T2DM. Insulin for T1DM and advanced T2DM. Consider GLP-1RA, SGLT2i, DPP-4i, TZDs based on comorbidities.
  • 🏥 Monitoring: HbA1c every 3–6 months, annual microvascular screening (retina, kidney, neuropathy).
  • ❤️ ASCVD risk reduction: statins, ACEi/ARB, antiplatelet if indicated.

🔷 Complications

Acute: DKA, HHS, severe hypoglycemia.
Chronic: Microvascular (retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy), macrovascular (CAD, stroke, PAD), diabetic foot, infections.

🔷 Children & Special Groups

Children with T1DM need insulin therapy and psychosocial support. T2DM in children is rising; treat with metformin/insulin if >10 years. Screen for thyroid, celiac disease, dyslipidemia, mental health.
